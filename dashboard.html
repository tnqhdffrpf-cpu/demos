Where things stand today

Day 30 of the conflict. The strait has been effectively closed since late February — only about 6 ships/day transit vs the normal 135. China, Russia, India, Iraq, Pakistan, Malaysia, and Thailand vessels have been granted passage by Iran as diplomatic signals.

Today: Iran reportedly accepted “most” of a 15-point US peace plan, per unnamed officials. Pakistan is playing mediator. But on Truth Social, Trump simultaneously threatened to destroy all of Iran’s oil infrastructure, power plants, and desalination facilities if Hormuz isn’t reopened immediately.

Watch: If a deal is confirmed this week, expect oil to drop sharply and markets to rally hard. If talks collapse, $130+ oil is back on the table and the correction resumes. The extended US deadline expires in roughly 10 days.
