FFT-Based Water Shaders: High-Fidelity Ocean Simulation Under 1ms on Modern GPUs
Game Developer Magazine • 2026

Modern water shader development in 2026 centers on FFT-based wave simulation: pre-computing wave data via Fast Fourier Transforms and applying inverse transforms per-frame to generate realistic, tiling ocean surfaces in sub-millisecond GPU time. Combined with dynamic caustics rendering, screen-space reflections, and adaptive LOD, developers can achieve cinematic water quality without meaningful frame-time budget. The technique is now accessible in Three.js via community shaders and in Unreal Engine 5.4's Water plugin.
