🔥 Big Story of the Week

The Iran war & Strait of Hormuz crisis — the story that won't leave the front page.

Eight weeks into a U.S.-Israeli air campaign, Iran's retaliatory closure of the Strait of Hormuz has throttled 20% of global crude flows down to single-digit daily tanker crossings. The IEA chief called it "the biggest energy security threat in history" — 13M barrels/day gone with no clear off-ramp. This week added the Lebanon ceasefire extension (a stabilizer) and Iranian FM Araghchi landing in Pakistan for back-channel talks (a maybe-stabilizer).

Why it matters & where it goes: This grows before it fades. Oil is the economic transmission belt — every week the Strait stays throttled, inflation expectations reset higher and central banks lose rate-cut optionality. The Pakistan talks are the first real diplomatic motion since February; if they produce a second U.S.–Iran round, watch for a relief rally. If they collapse, $120 Brent is back on the table by summer.
