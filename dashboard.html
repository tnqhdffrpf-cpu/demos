🔥 Big Story of the Week

U.S.–Iran Ceasefire: Markets Whipsaw, Strait of Hormuz Still in Play

The dominant story this week is the U.S.–Iran conflict and its evolving ceasefire. After Trump threatened to destroy Iranian infrastructure unless the Strait of Hormuz reopened, Pakistan brokered a tentative two-week suspension of hostilities. Markets celebrated Wednesday with the Dow's best day in a year and crude crashing 16% — only for oil to reverse course Thursday as analysts noted Iran still effectively controls the strait.

Why it matters: The Strait of Hormuz handles roughly 20% of global oil throughput. Even a temporary disruption ripples through energy prices, shipping, and inflation expectations worldwide. This story has strong staying power — two weeks of ceasefire means the next deadline falls right around April 23, guaranteeing continued market sensitivity.

Verdict: This grows. The ceasefire is fragile, oil remains above $95, and markets will reprice every headline until a durable deal emerges.
