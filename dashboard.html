🧭 Big Story of the Week

U.S.–Iran war and the Strait of Hormuz shutdown

Every major outlet is leading with this: after Saturday's Iranian attack on a tanker and Sunday's U.S. seizure of an Iranian cargo ship, the ceasefire that has held since February's war is hanging by a thread. Iran's foreign ministry says talks are off the table, and the Strait of Hormuz — which carries roughly a fifth of seaborne oil — has been effectively closed for nearly two months.

Why it matters: The ceasefire agreement expires this week. If it lapses without renewal, expect oil back above $100, a hit to global equities, and real pressure on the administration to either negotiate or escalate. Markets are priced for de-escalation; the news flow is pointing the other way.

Prediction: This story grows, not fades. There's no clean off-ramp before the ceasefire deadline, and both sides have incentives to look tough for domestic audiences. Watch oil and the VIX for the early tell.
